🧠 Mental health · 4

Screening for depression, anxiety, stress and burnout

🌧️

Depression self-check

PHQ-9 style depression screening
9 items

A nine-item depression screening check based on the symptom criteria used in primary care worldwide. It asks how often each core depressive symptom has bothered you over the past two weeks.

  • Mood — Feeling down, hopeless, or losing interest in things you used to enjoy
  • Physical — Sleep disturbance, fatigue, and appetite change
  • Self-view — Guilt, worthlessness, and feeling like a failure
  • Function — Concentration, and being noticeably slowed down or restless
  • + 1 more areas
🌀

Anxiety self-check

GAD-7 style anxiety screening
7 items

A seven-item screening check for generalised anxiety, covering excessive worry, tension and fearfulness over the past two weeks.

  • Worry — Uncontrollable worry across many different topics
  • Tension — Nervousness, trouble relaxing, and restlessness
  • Irritability — Becoming easily annoyed or on edge
  • Apprehension — A sense that something bad is about to happen
🪨

Perceived stress check

PSS-10 perceived stress
10 items

A ten-item measure of perceived stress — not how many stressful things happened to you, but how uncontrollable and overloaded your life has felt in the past month.

  • Unpredictability — Being caught off guard by things you did not expect
  • Loss of control — Feeling unable to influence what matters to you
  • Overload — Having more to do than you can cope with
  • Coping (reversed) — Confidence, feeling on top of things, and managing irritations
🔥

Burnout check

CBI personal burnout
6 items

A six-item measure of personal burnout: the degree of physical and psychological exhaustion you are experiencing, independent of its cause.

  • Physical exhaustion — Tiredness, weakness, and getting ill more easily
  • Emotional exhaustion — Feeling emotionally drained and worn out
  • Depletion — The sense of having nothing left to give

🪞 Self-understanding · 2

Self-esteem and resilience

🌱

Self-esteem scale

Rosenberg self-esteem
10 items

A ten-item measure of global self-worth — how positively or negatively you regard yourself overall.

  • Self-worth — Believing you are a person of value, equal to others
  • Self-satisfaction — Being broadly content with who you are
  • Competence — Feeling able to do things as well as most people
  • Self-criticism (reversed) — Feeling useless, having little to be proud of, or feeling like a failure
🌊

Resilience check

Brief Resilience Scale
6 items

A six-item measure of one narrow but important thing: your ability to bounce back and recover after stress.

  • Recovery speed — How quickly you return to normal after something difficult
  • Coping under strain — Getting through stressful events without being derailed
  • Rumination (reversed) — How long setbacks stay with you

📵 Habits & dependence · 4

Social media, smartphone, gaming and alcohol habits

📱

Social media habit check

Bergen social media scale
6 items

A six-item check for problematic social media use, built on the standard components model of behavioural addiction.

  • Salience — Thinking about social media when you are not using it
  • Tolerance — Needing to use it more and more
  • Mood modification — Using it to escape personal problems
  • Relapse & withdrawal — Failing to cut down, and feeling restless without it
  • + 1 more areas
📵

Smartphone dependence check

SAS-SV
10 items

A ten-item self-check for smartphone dependence, focused on interference with daily life and loss of control rather than raw screen time.

  • Daily-life disturbance — Missed work, poor concentration, and physical strain
  • Withdrawal — Impatience and restlessness without your phone
  • Preoccupation — Thinking about your phone when you are not on it
  • Tolerance — Using it longer than you intended, again and again
🎮

Gaming habit check

DSM-5 IGD criteria
9 items

A nine-item self-check adapted from the nine proposed criteria for internet gaming disorder. It asks about control and consequences, not how many hours you play.

  • Preoccupation — Thinking about gaming when doing other things
  • Withdrawal & tolerance — Irritability without gaming, and needing more of it
  • Loss of control — Failed attempts to cut down, and deception about play time
  • Consequences — Lost interests, and risk to relationships, work or study
🍺

Alcohol use self-check

WHO AUDIT
10 items

The World Health Organization's ten-item screening questionnaire for hazardous and harmful drinking.

  • Consumption — Frequency, typical quantity, and heavy drinking episodes
  • Dependence — Impaired control, morning drinking, and salience
  • Harm — Guilt, blackouts, injuries, and concern from other people
