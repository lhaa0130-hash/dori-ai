Privacy Policy

illo takes the protection of your personal data seriously.

Last updated: July 4, 2026

Article 1 (Purposes of Processing Personal Data)+
The Company processes personal data for the purposes set out below. Personal data being processed is not used for any purpose other than those below, and if the purpose of use changes, the Company will take the necessary measures, such as obtaining separate consent under Article 18 of the Personal Information Protection Act. ① Membership registration and management • Verifying intent to register, and identifying and authenticating members for membership-based services • Maintaining and managing membership status, and preventing fraudulent use of the service • Various notices and communications, and handling grievances ② Service provision • Providing AI tool information, and insight and guide content • Operating community board services and receiving and handling suggestions • Providing personalized services and identity verification ③ Service improvement and statistical analysis • Understanding service usage and performing statistical analysis • Developing new services and improving existing ones • Analyzing access frequency and compiling statistics on users' use of the service
Article 2 (Items of Personal Data Collected and Methods of Collection)+
① Mandatory items [At sign-up] • Email address, password, nickname (name) [Collected automatically during service use] • Service usage records, access logs, access IP address • Browser type, operating system, device information • Cookies ② Optional items • Profile image, gender, age range • When submitting a suggestion: name, email ③ Methods of collection • Direct entry by the user at sign-up through the website • Automatic generation and collection during use of the service • Client-side data storage via localStorage ※ The Company does not collect sensitive data (such as ideology, beliefs or health information) or unique identifying information (such as resident registration numbers) without the user's prior consent.
Article 3 (Processing and Retention Periods)+
① The Company processes and retains personal data within the retention and use period required by law, or within the retention and use period consented to by the data subject at the time of collection. ② The processing and retention period for each category is as follows. • Member information: until the member withdraws from membership (However, where an investigation or inquiry is under way due to a violation of applicable law, until that investigation or inquiry concludes) ③ Retention periods required by applicable law • Records on contracts or withdrawal of subscription: 5 years (Act on the Consumer Protection in Electronic Commerce) • Records on payment and supply of goods: 5 years (Act on the Consumer Protection in Electronic Commerce) • Records on consumer complaints or dispute resolution: 3 years (Act on the Consumer Protection in Electronic Commerce) • Records on labelling and advertising: 6 months (Act on the Consumer Protection in Electronic Commerce) • Website visit records: 3 months (Protection of Communications Secrets Act)
Article 4 (Provision of Personal Data to Third Parties)+
① As a rule, the Company processes users' personal data only within the scope of the purposes specified in Article 1, and does not process it beyond that original scope or provide it to third parties without the user's prior consent. ② The following cases are exceptions. • Where the user has consented in advance to provision to a third party • Where there is a special provision in law, or where it is unavoidable in order to comply with a legal obligation • Where the data subject or their legal representative is unable to express intent, or cannot give prior consent due to an unknown address, and provision is clearly necessary for the urgent life, bodily or property interests of the data subject or a third party • Where it is necessary for purposes such as compiling statistics or academic research, and the personal data is provided in a form in which no specific individual can be identified
Article 5 (Outsourcing of Personal Data Processing)+
① To provide the service smoothly, the Company outsources personal data processing as follows. • Processor (trustee): Cloudflare, Inc. • Scope of outsourced work: website hosting and content delivery (CDN) ② When entering into an outsourcing contract, the Company specifies in the contract or other document — in accordance with Article 26 of the Personal Information Protection Act — matters such as the prohibition on processing personal data for purposes other than performing the outsourced work, technical and administrative safeguards, restrictions on sub-outsourcing, supervision of the trustee, and liability including damages, and supervises whether the trustee processes personal data safely. ③ If the content of the outsourced work or the trustee changes, the Company will disclose the change through this privacy policy without delay.

Article 7 (Procedures and Methods for Destroying Personal Data)+
① When personal data becomes unnecessary — for example because the retention period has elapsed or the purpose of processing has been achieved — the Company destroys it without delay. ② Where personal data must continue to be preserved under other laws even though the consented retention period has elapsed or the purpose of processing has been achieved, the Company moves that personal data to a separate database or stores it in a different location. ③ Procedures and methods of destruction • Procedure: the Company selects the personal data for which grounds for destruction have arisen and destroys it with the approval of the personal information protection officer. • Methods - Electronic files: destroyed using a technical method that makes the records unrecoverable. - Personal data printed on paper: shredded or incinerated. - Data stored in localStorage: client-side stored data is deleted together with the account when the account is deleted.
Article 8 (Rights and Obligations of Data Subjects and Legal Representatives, and How to Exercise Them)+
① Users (data subjects) may exercise the following data protection rights against the Company at any time. • Request to access personal data • Request to correct errors • Request for deletion • Request to suspend processing ② These rights may be exercised in writing, by email and similar means, and the Company will act on such requests without delay. ③ Rights may also be exercised through a representative, such as a legal representative or a duly authorized agent. ④ For a child under the age of 14, the legal representative may request access to, correction or deletion of, or suspension of the processing of the child's personal data.
Article 9 (Personal Data of Children Under 14)+
① illo (the "Company") provides its service in principle to persons aged 14 or older, and membership registration is available only to those aged 14 or older. ② Where a child under the age of 14 wishes to register as a member or use the service, consent must be obtained from a legal representative (such as a parent) pursuant to Article 22-2 of the Personal Information Protection Act. The Company may collect minimal information from the child, such as the legal representative's name and contact details, in order to obtain that consent. ③ Methods of verifying the legal representative's consent • Confirming intent to consent via the legal representative's email or other contact details • Verification through a separate consent confirmation procedure established by the Company • Other procedures equivalent to methods prescribed by applicable law When processing the personal data of a child under 14, the Company verifies whether the legal representative's consent has been obtained, and where consent cannot be confirmed, destroys the personal data without delay. ④ Rights of the legal representative • The legal representative may at any time request access to, correction or deletion of, or suspension of processing of the child's personal data, and may withdraw consent. • Where the legal representative withdraws consent, the Company takes the necessary measures without delay. ⑤ To protect the personal data of children under 14, the Company does not advertise to children goods or services that require their legal representative's consent, nor does it improperly induce children to provide personal data. ※ Children under 14 or their legal representatives may direct inquiries about a child's personal data to the personal information protection officer at illo@illo.im.
Article 10 (Measures to Ensure the Security of Personal Data)+
The Company takes the following measures to ensure the security of personal data. ① Administrative measures • Establishing and implementing an internal management plan • Minimizing and training the staff who handle personal data ② Technical measures • Encryption of personal data: users' passwords are stored and managed in encrypted form. • Technical countermeasures against hacking and similar threats: installation of security software with periodic updates and inspections • Access control: restricting access rights to personal data and taking the measures necessary to manage them • Use of HTTPS (SSL/TLS) encrypted communication to keep data secure in transit ③ Physical measures • Physical access control through Cloudflare's global security infrastructure
Article 11 (Cookies and Automatic Collection Devices)+
① The Company uses cookies to make the service more convenient for users. ② Purposes of using cookies • Analyzing access frequency and visit duration in order to improve the service • Identifying users' areas of interest in order to provide personalized services • Maintaining login state (using localStorage) ③ Types of cookies • Essential cookies: cookies that are indispensable to providing the service • Analytics cookies: cookies used for statistical analysis of service usage • Advertising cookies: cookies used to deliver personalized advertising ④ Advertising services The Company may use third-party advertising services including Google AdSense. For details on Google's handling of advertising-related personal data, please refer to the Google Privacy Policy (policies.google.com/privacy). ⑤ How to refuse cookies You may refuse the storage of cookies through your web browser settings. Please note that refusing cookies may make some parts of the service difficult to use.
Article 12 (Personal Information Protection Officer and Responsible Department)+
① The Company designates a personal information protection officer and a responsible department as set out below, to take overall responsibility for personal data processing and to handle users' inquiries, complaints and damage relief relating to personal data processing. ▶ Personal Information Protection Officer (CPO) • Position: illo Operations Lead • Email: illo@illo.im ▶ Personal data protection department • Department: illo Operations Team • Email: illo@illo.im ※ The officer's name is not disclosed in order to protect personal data; inquiries and processing are handled through the position, responsible department and the representative contact (email). ② Users may direct any inquiries, complaints or requests for damage relief relating to personal data protection that arise while using the Company's service to the personal information protection officer or the responsible department. The Company will respond to and handle such inquiries without delay. ③ The Company faithfully performs the responsibilities and role of the personal information protection officer in accordance with applicable law regarding personal data processing.

① This privacy policy applies from February 16, 2026. ② Where the Company amends this privacy policy, it will continuously disclose the timing of the change and its entry into force together with the amended content.
